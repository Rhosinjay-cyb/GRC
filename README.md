Firstly, I conducted the risk assessment of an organization's procurement process, identifying key control deficiencies, including the absence of a formal cash disbursement policy, inadequate access control measures and verbal approval for privileged access among other weaknesses. Based on these findings, I recommended appropriate security and governance controls to strengthen the organization's control environment and support compliance with the Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) act.\\
The second task involved conducting an audit walkthrough of a client's software development lifecycle (SDLC) to identify security gaps and evaluate the effectiveness of the existing development controls. Based on the assessment, I recommended implementing formal design reviews before the implementation phase to ensure the system design meets the minimum security control requirements provided in the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) special publications, NIST SP 800-53.\
The final task involved assessing the design and operating effectiveness of an organization's change management controls. The assessment identified deficiencies in two key control areas: emergency change management and segregation of duties. A review of a sample change management request form revealed that an emergency change was approved by the requestor rather than the IT Director, and the change advisory board (CAB) failed to provide the required post-implementation approval within the prescribed five-day period. To strengthen the control environment, I recommended implementing a weekly review of all implemented changes to verify that emergency changes receive the appropriate approvals in accordance with the organization's change management policy.\
Additionally, the assessment identified a segregation of duties conflict, where a single user had been assigned both developer and implementer roles, creating a significant change management risk by allowing the same individual to develop and deploy changes to production. To mitigate this risk, I recommended periodic access reviews to ensure these roles remain appropriately segregated and assigned to different individuals.\ 
This simulation not only indicates the significance of cybersecurity frameworks and regulatory standards but also highlighted the critical role of IT general Controls (ITGCs) in implementing and sustaining effective security and compliance organization-wide. Likewise, it also underscored the collaborative nature of cybersecurity, demonstrating that addressing complex security challenges requires the combined expertise of professionals across multiple security domains.\
